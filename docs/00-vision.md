# 00 — Vision

## One-line description

Reality Skin Engine lets a user look at the real world through a phone, headset, or future AR glasses and switch the visible environment into another era, future, mood, or fictional interpretation.

## Core idea

The system does not need to replace the real world. It needs to understand enough of the real world to visually reinterpret it.

```text
Real environment
  → camera feed
  → depth / plane / mesh understanding
  → object and surface labeling
  → era-pack rules
  → visual overlay
  → user sees a reskinned world
```

## Examples

### 1812 mode

- Roads become dirt or stone.
- Modern signs become painted wood signs.
- Streetlights become lanterns or torches.
- Buildings receive brick, timber, or weathered plaster overlays.
- Ambient audio shifts to horses, wind, carts, and low town noise.

### 1500s mode

- Buildings become timber, stone, plaster, thatch, or fortress-like surfaces.
- Vehicles are hidden or stylized behind carts, wagons, or market props.
- Metal and plastic surfaces are reduced.
- Signs become carved, painted, or cloth banners.

### 3055 mode

- Roads become glowing transit grids.
- Buildings become glass, carbon, metal, and luminous megastructure skins.
- Signs become holographic panels.
- Cars are visually wrapped as autonomous pods.
- Drones, orbital lines, and sky overlays can be added as optional props.

## Why this is not just another AR app

Most AR apps place isolated objects into reality.

Reality Skin Engine changes the interpretation layer of reality:

```text
ordinary AR:      place object into world
Reality Skin:     reinterpret the world itself
```

The user remains grounded in the actual environment, but the visual meaning changes.

## Product categories

The same engine can support multiple markets:

- Education: history, architecture, archaeology, science visualization.
- Museums: city-scale historical overlays and guided interpretation.
- Tourism: location-aware historical walks.
- Games: real-world adventure, survival, mystery, creature hunts.
- Film: previsualization, blocking, and cinematic set extension.
- Wellness: calming world skins, forest overlays, low-stimulation views.
- Creator tools: user-made era packs and reality skins.

## Long-term direction

The long-term product is a spatial AI layer that understands the environment and renders alternative interpretations. The near-term product is a phone-based proof that shows the same real scene switching between two or more visual eras.

## Non-goals for the first prototype

- No custom AR glasses.
- No live EEG dependency.
- No full city-scale map.
- No perfect AI object recognition.
- No real-time generation for every surface.
- No multiplayer.

Those are later layers after the world-reskin loop is proven.
